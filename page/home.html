Diacritics

Accent aigu é over e, indicates uniquely the sound /e/ répéter, bébé
Accent grave à, è, ù over an e, indicates the sound /ɛ/ mère, frère
Accent circonflexe â, ê, î, ô, û over a, e and o, indicates the sound /ɑ/, /ɛ/ and /o/ (often, it indicates the historical deletion of an adjacent letter) prêtre, théâtre
Tréma ë, ï, ü, ÿ indicates that a vowel is to be pronounced separately from the preceding one naïve /naiv/, Noël /nɔɛl/
Cédille ç under c, indicates that it is pronounced /s/ rather than /k/ français, ça va

Ligatures

E dans l'O Œ This ligature is a mandatory contraction of ⟨oe⟩ in certain words. Some of these are native French words, with the pronunciation /œ/ or /ø/.
Also used in words of Greek origin, as the Latin rendering of the Greek diphthong οι, with the pronunciation /e/.		 sœur /sœʁ/, œuvre /œvʁ/
œsophage /ezɔfaʒ/, Œdipe /edip/
E dans l'A Æ This ligature is rare, appearing only in some words of Latin and Greek origin. It generally represents the vowel /e/, like ⟨é⟩. tænia, ex æquo, cæcum, æthuse
